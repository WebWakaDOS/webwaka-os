### 1. CONTEXT AND GOALS

WebWaka is a multi‑vertical SaaS platform (Commerce, Civic, Transport, POS, etc.) with an existing, already‑defined plan to introduce AI capabilities across the ecosystem via a **BYOK (Bring Your Own Key)** model. However, that original plan *did not* include WebWaka being an AI service provider in its own right.

Now, WebWaka wants to add a new long‑term layer:

- **WebWaka SuperAgent**: a credit‑based AI orchestration platform that:
  - Sits as a unified layer across all WebWaka products and services.
  - Uses **AI aggregators** such as OpenRouter, Together, Eden, and similar providers (no direct relationships with individual LLM vendors).
  - Provides:
    - Chat, agents, automations, media generation, and long‑running agentive workflows.
    - A **universal, prepaid credit system** for all WebWaka users.
    - A **default SuperAgent API key** for every WebWaka user once AI is enabled; this key becomes the default in the BYOK model.
    - Tiered access (Free, Pro, Business, Enterprise) with partner‑resale of credit packs and bundles.
  - Acts as:
    - The **front‑end, routing layer, and UX**.
    - The **billing and wallet system** (credits, top‑ups, usage metering).
    - The **policy and safety layer** (rate limits, spend caps, audit logs, content filters, human‑in‑the‑loop).

Your objective is to:
- Deeply integrate WebWaka SuperAgent into the **long‑term WebWaka platform architecture**.
- Update **all existing plans, documents, and implementation timelines** so that the AI strategy is now centered on SuperAgent, while still preserving and aligning with the original BYOK concept.
- Ensure that every plan is designed so that **future agents executing tasks will always**:
  - Do a deep research into all relevant documents and code.
  - Do thorough online research to follow best practices.
  - Implement strictly according to the updated, consolidated plans.
  - Not drift or create divergent, outdated artifacts.

This is **far more important than speed**. Complete, synthesized, up‑to‑date alignment across all documents and plans is the top priority.

### 3. NEW PLAN STRUCTURE

Now that you have synthesized everything, you will **produce a new, comprehensive, and unified execution and implementation plan** for WebWaka. This plan must:

- Fully integrate **WebWaka SuperAgent** into:
  - The **long‑term platform infrastructure**.
  - The **existing AI integration plan** (including the original BYOK concept).
- Cut across **all layers**:
  - Pre‑vertical setups (infrastructure, auth, billing, data, observability).
  - Verticals (Commerce, Civic, Transport, POS, etc.) and their specific AI use cases.
- Ensure that:
  - All future AI work in WebWaka **must route through SuperAgent** unless explicitly and temporarily allowed otherwise (e.g., legacy paths during migration).
  - The **default setting** for any AI‑enabled feature is:
    - Use the **WebWaka SuperAgent API key** (managed by WebWaka credits).
    - Allow the user/org to switch to **BYOK** (their own aggregator or direct provider key) where allowed by terms and where it makes sense.

The plan must be structured as follows:

#### 3.1. Overall architectural vision

Draft a **high‑level, long‑term architecture** for WebWaka that includes SuperAgent as a core layer. Include:

- How **identity, tenants, and orgs** interact with SuperAgent.
- How **pre‑vertical** services (auth, wallets, billing, observability) integrate with SuperAgent.
- How **each vertical** plugs into SuperAgent (e.g., Commerce for order assistants, POS for receipt‑based agents, Civic for case‑handling bots, etc.).
- How **credit‑based pricing and BYOK** are represented in the architecture.

Clearly show that SuperAgent is **not a standalone microservice** but a **centralized platform layer** like auth or billing.

#### 3.2. Product and feature spec for WebWaka SuperAgent

Create a **detailed product spec** for WebWaka SuperAgent that:

- Defines:
  - Core capabilities: chat, agents, automations, media generation, workflows.
  - Access tiers: Free, Pro, Business, Enterprise (with feature and quota differences).
  - Credit system:
    - What is a “WebWaka credit”.
    - How different workloads (tokens, images, audio, tool calls, workflow steps) map into credits.
    - How **aggregator‑level pricing** (OpenRouter, Together, Eden, etc.) is mapped into your internal credit model.
  - BYOK model:
    - How the **WebWaka‑managed SuperAgent key** is the default once AI is enabled.
    - How users can bring their **own aggregator keys** (e.g., OpenRouter, Together) where allowed.
    - Any platform‑fee or flat‑fee model WebWaka may charge.
  - Partner‑resale model:
    - How partners can sell credit packs and bundles.
    - How credits are scoped to end‑customer orgs, not to partners.
- Is aligned with:
  - Existing WebWaka product language and UX.
  - Existing AI‑related features and flows in all verticals.

#### 3.3. System architecture of WebWaka SuperAgent

Produce a **technical architecture RFC** for WebWaka SuperAgent, including:

- High‑level layers:
  - Identity & Tenants.
  - Wallet & Billing.
  - Routing Gateway.
  - Aggregator Abstraction (for OpenRouter, Together, Eden, etc.).
  - Agent Runtime.
  - Usage Meter.
  - Policy Engine.
  - UI/SDK.
- Data model:
  - Core entities: Accounts, Users, Wallets, CreditTransactions, ProviderKeys (for aggregators), Agents, UsageEvents, Policies, etc.
  - Simple schema sketches that can be turned into actual DB migrations later.
- API contracts:
  - At least a sketch of the main `/v1/superagent` endpoints (e.g., chat, agent run, workflow, BYOK‑attach).
  - How existing WebWaka modules will call SuperAgent.
- Aggregator‑aware routing:
  - How routing policies decide which aggregator and model to use.
  - How fallbacks and SLAs are handled.
  - How BYOK on aggregators is modeled.

Ensure this RFC explicitly references the **aggregator‑only model** (no direct relationships with OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.).

#### 3.4. Phased execution roadmap

Create a **multi‑phase, long‑term execution roadmap** that:

- Spans **at least 12–24 months**.
- Has **clear phases** such as:
  - Phase 1: Core SuperAgent API and wallet, basic chat, single aggregator.
  - Phase 2: Multi‑aggregator support, richer agent features, basic workflows.
  - Phase 3: Media generation, advanced policies, partner‑resale console.
  - Phase 4: Enterprise‑grade controls, observability, and deep integration across all verticals.
- For each phase:
  - List **pre‑vertical** tasks (e.g., auth hooks, billing integrations, observability).
  - List **vertical‑specific** tasks (what each vertical must do to adopt SuperAgent).
  - Define **dependencies** and **order**.
- Ensure that:
  - Existing AI‑related plans and timelines are **merged into this roadmap**, not duplicated.
  - Any **outdated or conflicting** plans are clearly marked as deprecated or replaced.

Update the **timelines** in all existing planning documents so that:
- The **new plan becomes the source of truth** for AI and SuperAgent.
- No old AI‑integration plan ever overrides the new one unless explicitly stated.

#### 3.5. Document and plan update mandates

Generate a **comprehensive document update plan** that:

- Lists **every file and document** that must be reviewed and updated (from your earlier synthesis).
- For each document:
  - State:
    - What must change (e.g., “replace direct OpenAI calls with SuperAgent routing”).

  - Assign:

    - They must perform **online research** into aggregators, best practices, and pricing.
    - They must implement strictly according to the **updated consolidated plan**, not any local or outdated sketch.
- Emphasize that:
  - **No outdated AI‑integration information** should remain in the codebase or docs.
  - If there is any ambiguity, the agent must flag and ask for clarification, not assume.

#### 3.6. Governance and consistency rules

Add a **governance and consistency section** that defines:

- How **new AI features** must be designed:
  - They must route through WebWaka SuperAgent if possible.
  - Direct calls to aggregators or providers are highly discouraged unless explicitly allowed.

  - When a user/org enables AI, the **WebWaka‑managed SuperAgent key** is the default.
